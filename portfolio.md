# Shotmaker and Daydreamer

<strong>[Daydreamer Video Clips](https://playcraftai.com/daydreamer-clips/)</strong>

<strong>[Daydreamer Experiments Breakdown](https://playcraftai.com/videos/)</strong>

Role: Founder, developer, creative technologist

Shotmaker is my current AI video and filmmaking tool project. The goal is to make AI video generation more controllable, repeatable, and useful for real storytelling.

Daydreamer grew out of that work as a short-form regenerative AI video showcase. It explores what happens when structured 3D scene data, feedback loops, prompts, masks, and music-driven parameter changes are combined into a single rendering system.

The project demonstrates:

- 3D-aware AI video workflows
- Music-driven rendering and beat-synchronized parameter automation
- Feedback-based visual evolution across frames
- Short-form AI video clips designed for social sharing
- Practical research toward more controllable AI filmmaking tools

# PeopleFun

<strong>[PeopleFun Website](https://www.peoplefun.com/)</strong>

Role: Applied AI consultant

I worked with PeopleFun as an AI consultant through PlayCraft AI on applied AI systems for word games, game design, player understanding, and dynamic difficulty.

The core idea was to better understand why players churn: whether a player was leaving because the game was too easy, too hard, or simply not matching the kind of challenge they wanted. From there, we explored ways to rate levels, model player behavior, and dynamically tune the experience so the game could better fit each player's play style. For word games in particular, semantic models and embeddings are a powerful way to reason about content, meaning, and player-specific tastes.

<strong>[Word Embeddings and the Shape of Meaning](https://playcraftai.com/word-embeddings/)</strong>

Areas of work included:

- Applied AI strategy for games
- Dynamic difficulty and player-fit modeling
- Level rating and difficulty analysis
- AI-assisted designer tools
- On-device model exploration
- Training and adapting models using studio data
- Prototyping workflows for improving game content, iteration, and decision-making
